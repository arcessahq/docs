# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Arcessa documentation should be a professional, practical, and respectful place for contributors and readers.

Examples of behavior that helps:

- Be direct and specific.
- Assume good intent while still being precise about problems.
- Focus critique on the content, not the person.
- Prefer actionable suggestions over vague feedback.
- Keep examples free of real secrets, customer data, and private infrastructure details.

Examples of behavior that is not acceptable:

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Sharing credentials, tokens, or customer data.
- Repeated low-effort noise that derails documentation work.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately to the maintainers. If the report involves a security vulnerability or secret exposure, follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) instead.

Maintainers may remove content, close discussions, or restrict participation when needed to keep the project useful and safe.
